About Us

Welcome to our IoT Analytics Project, developed as part of our BEE 3033 Data Communication and Networks course. This project highlights the power of Internet of Things (IoT) and cloud-based data analytics by integrating ThingSpeak to collect, analyze, and visualize real-time sensor data. Our goal is to demonstrate using wireless communication to provide real-time insights for informed decision-making. Through this project, we explore automated data processing, mathematical analysis using MATLAB, and cloud-based visualization, emphasizing the role of smart technology in data-driven solutions.

For our porject, we have chosen public channels from ThingSpeak that gathers data on air pollution. Hence, our system focuses on monitoring key environmental parameters such as Particulate Matter (PM2.5 and PM10). By analyzing these real-time data streams, we aim to provide valuable insights into air quality trends and their potential impact. Our implementation involves retrieving sensor data from ThingSpeak’s open-source cloud platform, which ar the data from the public channels. This approach allows us to interpret air quality fluctuations effectively and explore potential insights from the colelcted data.







Project Objectives

Objective #1

To collect real-time data from a public ThingSpeak channel.

Objective #2

To analyze the collected data using theoretical mathematical equations.

Objective #3

To display the analyzed results on a web page.
